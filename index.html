A comparison of house prices and real disposable income in the UK.

House Prices vs Disposable Income

Housing and home ownership, especially with regards to younger people, have caused many a heated debate in recent years. *More text*. In his latest book James O'Brien (2018) argues that one of the greatest challenges young people face on their way to home ownership is the widening gap between house prices and income - evidence suggests that he (and many others who have vocalised this) has got a point. Let's have a look.

Below you can see a chart that explores the relationship between house prices (Source: Nationwide Building Society) and real disposable income per capita (Source: Office for National Statistics), in the United Kingdom, from 1955 to 2017.

{{plot}}

Since the mid-1970s, house prices have generally grown much faster than real disposable income per capita, making it ever more difficult for individuals to buy homes.
